Dimensions represent different aspects or categories of data that you can analyze. Common examples of dimensions include **Time**, **Region**, **Product line**, and **Department**.

They let you break down and explore metrics across multiple perspectives, which supports multi-dimensional analysis.

## **Adding a dimension**

![](/images/getting-started/adding-dimension.png)

A new dimension (sometimes called a **category**) can be added to a variable.

## **Working with dimensions**

**Creating and linking dimensions**

Dimensions help you organize and structure your data in Oculon so you can analyze and break down information more easily. Whether you work with **departments**, **teams**, or **products**, dimensions keep that structure consistent. Here is how to set them up.

### **Creating dimensions**

**Create a dimension from the library**

Click **Create dimension**, enter a dimension name, and start adding dimension values.

![](/images/getting-started/create-dimension.png)

**Create a dimension from a variable**

Next to a variable, click **Add dimension**, then **Add new dimension**, and name the dimension to create it.

**Create a dimension from pages in a model**

Expand the **model pages** bar, click **+** next to **Dimensions**, and create a new dimension.

![](/images/getting-started/add-dim-pages.png)

## **Dimension breakdowns**

Dimension breakdowns let you divide a variable by items such as **Departments**, **Regions**, or **Products** so the same metric is more detailed and easier to act on. You set a formula once at the total; it applies to every dimension item automatically.

![](/images/getting-started/dimension-breakdown.png)

**Breaking down a variable by dimension**

- Click a variable and open **Dimensions**.
- Choose a dimension (e.g. **Region**). The variable breaks into multiple items (e.g. Asia, Europe, North America).

**Set a formula at the aggregate level**

- Add a formula on the top **(aggregate)** row of the variable.
- That formula is applied to all dimension items—you do not need to copy and paste it for each row.
