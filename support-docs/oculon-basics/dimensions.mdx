# Dimensions

Dimensions represent different aspects or categories of data that you can analyse. Common examples of dimensions include Time, Region, Product Line, or Department.

They enable users to break down and explore metrics across multiple perspectives, supporting multi-dimensional analysis.

## **Working with dimensions:**

Creating and Linking Dimensions Made Simple

Dimensions help you organize and structure your data in Oculon, making it easier to analyze and break down information. Whether you're working with departments, teams, or products, Dimensions keep things nice and tidy! Here's how to set them up.

### Creating Dimensions 📂

**Add a New Category**

Click the + icon on the left panel of your model.

Name your new Dimension and start adding items!

[image]

**Create a Dimension from a Variable**

Open a variable, click on Dimensions, and create a Dimension breakdown on the fly.

**Generate Dimensions from Data**

If your data source already has Dimensions (e.g., "Team" or "Vendor"), you can create them in Oculon with one click.

Connect your data to a variable and click Dimensions

Select an existing category from your data—it will automatically create and add it to your model.

## **Dimension Breakdowns:**

Dimension breakdowns let you divide a variable by items like **Departments, Regions, or Products**—making your data more detailed and actionable. The best part? You can set a formula once, and it applies to all dimension items automatically!

[image]

**Breaking Down a Variable by Dimension**

- Click on a variable and hit **Dimensions**.
- Choose a dimension (e.g., Region), and your variable will now be broken into multiple items (e.g., Asia, Europe, North America).

**Set a Formula at the Aggregate Level**

- Add a formula at the top (aggregate) row of the variable.
- This formula is automatically applied to all individual dimension items—no need to copy and paste it everywhere!
