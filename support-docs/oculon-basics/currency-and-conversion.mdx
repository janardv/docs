When working with numbers in Oculon, sometimes you’re dealing with different currencies—USD here, EUR there, maybe INR for that new market you’re entering. Oculon makes this simple by letting you set a currency for your variables and automatically converting foreign exchange (FX) rates.

No manual calculations. No headaches. Just clean, consistent data.

[image]

## **Setting Currency for Variables**

1️⃣ **Pick a Currency**

When creating or editing a variable, switch its format to **Currency**.

- Choose from popular currencies like **USD, GBP, EUR, or INR**.
- Oculon will add the currency symbol (e.g., $, £, ₹) so you know exactly what you’re looking at.

2️⃣ **Why Does This Matter?**

It’s not just for looks. Setting a currency gives a clear indication of what each number represents—so when your variable says ₹10,000, you know it’s in INR, not USD.
