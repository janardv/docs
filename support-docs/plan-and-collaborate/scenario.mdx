# Scenario

A **scenario** represents a possible version or outcome of a financial model based on specific assumptions, variables, and conditions. Scenarios help you to analyze the potential impact of changes in key drivers (e.g., pricing, demand, costs) on business performance.

Scenarios can vary from **best-case and worst-case projections** to simulations of external events like market fluctuations or policy changes.

**Benefits of Scenarios**

1. **Enhanced Decision-Making**
    - Scenarios allow businesses to evaluate multiple outcomes before committing to a decision, reducing risks and improving confidence in strategy.
2. **Flexibility and Agility**
    - By simulating different possibilities, businesses can quickly adapt to changes and build contingency plans.
3. **Insightful Analysis**
    - Scenarios provide detailed insights into how specific variables impact the overall model, aiding in targeted action.
4. **Better Communication**
    - Clear, data-backed scenarios make it easier to communicate strategies and decisions to stakeholders.
5. **Scenario Comparison**
    - Users can compare different scenarios side-by-side to identify the most favorable outcomes or detect potential risks.

**Outcomes of Using Scenarios**

1. **Risk Mitigation**
    - By planning for multiple possibilities, user can anticipate and mitigate risks effectively.
2. **Optimized Resource Allocation**
    - Scenarios reveal the best ways to allocate budgets, personnel, and other resources under different conditions.
3. **Improved Financial Performance**
    - Proactively preparing for changes leads to better financial management and improved profitability.
4. **Future-Ready Strategies**
    - Scenarios enable businesses to prepare for market trends and disruptions, making them resilient and competitive.
5. **Stakeholder Confidence**
    - Presenting well-thought-out scenarios reassures investors, partners, and employees about the business’s preparedness and direction.

**Example in Oculon**

- You can create scenarios for different growth cases, visualize their impact on revenue and profit, and adjust assumptions to find the optimal pricing strategy as well as the Headcount and Hiring, Marketing strategy.

**How to work with Scenario in Oculon?**

You can create a or multiple scenario of a model from the Model Studio Page itself.

1. To create a scenario Go to your model from Model Studio and access the "Scenario" button at top right.
2. You an give a name to the Scenario to identify better:

Give your scenario a meaningful name to make it easy to identify later. Example: Growth Scenario, Worst Case Scenario

1. Work with scenario

Once a scenario is created, you can:

1. **Update Formulas**: Modify existing formulas to test different assumptions or conditions.
2. **Edit Cells**: Change specific data points to see how adjustments impact the model.
3. **Add Variables**: Introduce new variables to account for factors like inflation, seasonal demand, or operational changes.
4. **Add Dimensions**: Expand the scope of analysis by including new dimensions such as regions, product lines, or departments.

You can make changes to the scenario by updating the formula, cell, adding variable and dimensions.

1. Adding Multiple scenario

You can add multiple scenarios for a single model.

- You can create **multiple scenarios** for a single model to explore various possibilities side-by-side.
- Each scenario operates independently, allowing you to compare outcomes without altering the base model.
